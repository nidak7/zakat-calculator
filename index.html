Methodology

What is included, what is not, and why

This page is an educational aid, not a fatwa. It aims to stay simple while staying close to core texts and widely used contemporary guidance.

Usually excluded from wealth zakat

  • Your primary home and normal household furniture.
  • Your personal car, clothing, phones, and daily-use items.
  • Business equipment and fixed assets not held for sale.
  • Doubtful receivables you do not reasonably expect back.

Who can receive zakat

  • The poor.
  • The needy.
  • Those assigned to administer zakat.
  • Those whose hearts are to be reconciled.
  • For freeing people from bondage.
  • People in debt.
  • For the cause of Allah.
  • The stranded traveller.
